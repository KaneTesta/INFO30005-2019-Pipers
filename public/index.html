Helping contribute to Australia's waste epidemic

Australians discard up to 20% of the food they purchase. That equates to 1 out of every 5 bags of groceries and cost $9.6 billion dollars worth in food and drinks annually, equating to $1050 per household. While we waste a significant proportion of edible food we purchase, nearly 18% of Australians (or 4 million people) were reported to have faced food insecurity in 2018. There also seems to be a disparity between the communities who waste food the most per capita (urban centres) and the communities most vulnerable to food insecurity (regional and remote areas). While negligence by the end consumer results in high levels of food wastage, it is not limited to that.
